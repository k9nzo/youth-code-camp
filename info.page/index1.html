Understanding Depression

What is Depression?

Depression is a serious but treatable mental health condition that affects millions of people worldwide. It goes beyond feelings of sadness or having a bad day—it's a persistent feeling of emptiness, hopelessness, or despair that can last for weeks, months, or even years. Depression can impact every aspect of life, from work and relationships to physical health. Understanding depression is the first step in breaking the silence and removing the stigma surrounding it.

Facts and Statistics

  • Depression is a common mental disorder.
  • Depression can lead to suicide
  • Globally, an estimated 5% of adults suffer from depression
  • Approximately 280 million people in the world have depression (1). Depression is about 50% more common among women than among men.

Common Symptoms

  • Continuous low mood or sadness
  • Feeling irritable and intolerant of others
  • Having low self-esteem.

Identifying Depression

Recognizing the signs of depression is crucial for early intervention and support. Common symptoms include persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, or emptiness; a loss of interest in activities once enjoyed; changes in appetite or sleep patterns; and fatigue or lack of energy. People with depression may also experience difficulty concentrating, feelings of worthlessness or guilt, and even thoughts of self-harm or suicide. If these symptoms persist for more than two weeks, it’s important to seek help from a mental health professional. Early identification and treatment can make a significant difference in managing depression and improving quality of life.
